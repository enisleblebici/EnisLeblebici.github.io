About

A chemical engineer working on intensified processes and deployable AI.

Associate Professor, KU Leuven Dept. of Chemical Engineering Process Engineering for Sustainable Systems (ProcESS) Member of Leuven.AI

My work combines process engineering insight with data-driven methods. I develop and study intensified reactors and structures for energy-related applications, especially in photo(electro)chemistry and carbon capture, while also building AI methods that remain useful under industrial constraints such as uncertainty, limited data, and complex multiphase behaviour.

This website summarises my research themes, selected publications, and the ways I collaborate with industry through training, coaching, modelling, and optimisation projects.

Research

Two main research directions

1. Process intensification in energy and CCUS

Research on intensified reactors and structured systems for energy and carbon-related applications, with emphasis on reactor design, scale-up, and performance improvement.

  • Photochemical and electrochemical reactor design
  • Structure-domain intensification using aerosols, monoliths, and related architectures
  • CCUS-oriented process and reactor concepts
  • Transport, scale-up, and structure–performance relationships

2. AI in process intensification

Development of AI methods that support intensified process design, interpretation, and optimisation, while staying grounded in process knowledge.

  • Hybrid modelling and grey-box methods
  • AI-aided process and structure design
  • Structure optimisation and design-space exploration
  • Foundation-model-inspired approaches for chemical engineering problems

Representative topics

Photo(electro)chemical systems

Scalable structured reactors, illumination strategies, transport limitations, and reactor performance analysis.

Aerosols & monoliths

Structure-domain intensification, interfacial area engineering, and high-performance contacting concepts.

Deployable AI

Machine learning and hybrid approaches designed for uncertainty, sparse data, and industrial reality.
